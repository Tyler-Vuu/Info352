How we are combating the AI ethics issue

While the way we currently train AI can lead to a design with flaws, it is not an unsolvable problem and there are a few solutions that can lead to more ethical AI design.

The first step in creating more ethical AI is education. So much of the people creating AI is focused on the moving forward of the technology and it's these people that are often uneducated in the potential ethical issues of pushing forward as fast as possible. Programs created should “focus on ethical implications, potential risks, and the guidelines for data usage.”(Reineke) It’s crucial to build these fundamentals so that employees can navigate the complex nature of generative AI and know the potential ethical implications associated with their different decisions when creating ethical AI. The people creating it should not be the only ones to be educated on the benefits of AI as educating the public can also cover for the inevitable flaws of biased and misinformation of AI. In the mind of the general public, AI is a magic box that spits out answers and most will take the answers at face value leading to the spread of bias and misinformation. Giving transparency in the process of AI and educating the public on the shortcomings of the technology will lead to a more healthy relationship with AI.

The second solution involves fixing the workplace culture and diversity of AI companies. So much of the current AI workforce is made up of white males that voices of representation within these companies are often overlooked and concerns of AI bias are thrown aside in favor of progress. According to Joy Buolamwini, “Less than 2% of employees in technical roles at Facebook and Google are black and at eight large tech companies only around a fifth of the technical workforce at each are women.” (Buolamwini)It’s important for the people affected the most by the flaws in AI to be in the room while it’s being developed. The more diverse the workforce, the more that ethical design will find its way into AI.

Lastly, a good place to look at how to make AI more ethical is to fix the way that we train our AI. To create the large language models behind AI, enormous amounts of data are needed to train it and it’s through this process that the AI can come to racist or sexist conclusions through the data it receives. “Ensuring that the training data is fair, unbiased, and representative of diverse inputs is a critical part of maintaining ethical integrity in generative AI.”(Reineke). When one samples data from convenience and does not take into consideration the importance of unbiased training data, you get a biased AI that reinforces the stereotypes and suppression of minorities. Another problem with training AI has to do with the development of AI in first-world countries. Most large datasets suitable for machine learning training come from first-world countries and as a result, “AI-generated language will be homogenized, reflecting the practices of the richest countries and communities.”(Raikes) We must ensure that the future of AI is for everyone and not just a way for the richest countries to spread their influence further. While it may be difficult for these countries to build up the wealth of databases needed to train AIs, I think it’s important to acknowledge this shortcoming and find ways to make up for this shortcoming.
